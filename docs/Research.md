# A research-backed roadmap for a browser-native hypnosis tool

The single highest-impact upgrade is swapping the Web Speech API for **Kokoro-82M neural TTS** loaded from a CDN — it turns a robotic reader into a warm, hypnotic narrator in a single `<script>` tag. Beyond that, the research points to a consistent pattern: your tool already has more *breadth* (25 personas, 37 inductions) than any commercial app, but lacks the *depth* mechanics — structured multi-session programs, pre/post state tracking, re-alerting protocols, and single-voice coherence — that drive therapeutic outcomes and retention. The evidence base from 2022–2026 is strong enough to design around: Reveri's 84,395-user study (Tran et al., npj Digital Medicine 2026) shows pre→post stress reductions of **Cohen's d = −0.71 to −0.78** sustained across the first ten sessions, with interactive sessions outperforming listen-only. Nerva's gut-directed hypnotherapy RCT (Anderson et al., Am J Gastroenterol 2025) shows a digital 6-week protocol matches face-to-face therapy. Both use the same formula: short daily sessions, structured progression, and explicit pre/post measurement. A client-side localStorage tool can replicate that formula without a backend. The rest of this report translates each research dimension into prioritized, implementable changes.

## The clinical evidence is stronger than most app-builders assume

Recent meta-analyses put hypnotherapy on firm ground. Rosendahl et al.'s 2024 umbrella review (49 meta-analyses, *Frontiers in Psychology* 14:1330238) found **medium effect sizes (d ≈ 0.5–0.7)** across pain, IBS, anxiety, and sleep conditions. A 2025 Ericksonian hypnotherapy meta-analysis reported pooled SMD = 1.17 vs. waitlist and non-inferiority to CBT. This matters for your tool because it justifies offering condition-specific protocols without overclaiming. The research also reshapes design priorities: **De Pascalis et al. (2023) found formal inductions produced EEG changes largely equivalent to closed-eye relaxation in informed subjects — expectancy and rapport matter more than induction technique**. Your 37 inductions are already overkill; what's missing is the *scaffolding around* each one.

Three clinically-grounded features should move up your roadmap. First, add a **5-minute Hypnotic Induction Profile-style self-screener** at onboarding — eye-roll self-rating, arm-heaviness imagery, absorption items drawn from the Tellegen scale — validated in its full form by Alexander, Stimpson, Kittle & Spiegel (2021, *IJCEH* 69:72). Elkins Hypnotizability Scale–Clinical Form (Kekecs et al., 2016) offers a 9-item alternative with α = .78–.85 and r = .82–.86 with Stanford C. Store the score in localStorage and branch: higher scorers tolerate direct suggestions and vivid imagery; lower scorers benefit from indirect/Ericksonian framing and cognitive-reframe language. This single feature is Reveri's $100/year centerpiece.

Second, **implement the Howard Alertness Scale protocol** (Howard, 2017, *Am J Clin Hypn* 59:344). Capture a 0–10 alertness baseline before each session; re-check after; if post-score falls below baseline, auto-trigger a 60-second 5-4-3-2-1 grounding sequence before allowing navigation away. This is the single most evidence-based safety mechanism in digital hypnosis and no commercial app implements it explicitly. Third, design sessions around the **10–20 minute / daily / ~6-week** envelope that every successful protocol converges on (Nerva's 6-week IBS program, Elkins' 5-week menopause protocol, Reveri's 10-min interactive standard). Lally et al.'s 66-day habit-formation curve (2010, *Eur J Soc Psychol* 40:998) — with its explicit finding that **missing a single day does not derail habit formation** — should drive streak design.

## Why Kokoro-82M is the killer audio upgrade

The Web Speech API ceiling is low and uneven: Siri voices on macOS/iOS are excellent, Windows and Linux engines are robotic, and SSML prosody control is unreliably parsed across engines. **Kokoro-82M** (Apache 2.0, 82M-parameter StyleTTS2 derivative with ISTFTNet vocoder) closes the gap with commercial TTS and runs entirely client-side via `kokoro-js` and Transformers.js v3. The `af_heart` and `af_bella` voices are widely praised for warm, calming delivery — ideal for hypnosis cadence. Implementation is roughly ten lines:

```html
<script type="module">
import { KokoroTTS } from "https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/kokoro-js@1.2/dist/kokoro.web.js";
const tts = await KokoroTTS.from_pretrained(
  "onnx-community/Kokoro-82M-v1.0-ONNX",
  { dtype: navigator.gpu ? "fp32" : "q8", device: navigator.gpu ? "webgpu" : "wasm" }
);
</script>
```

The q8 WASM build is ~82 MB (cached in the Cache API after first load, persists across sessions), the fp32 WebGPU build is ~330 MB but 3–5× faster. WebGPU now ships by default in Chrome, Edge, Firefox 141+, and Safari 26 across macOS Tahoe/iOS 26/visionOS 26 — but ship a WASM fallback regardless. Gate it behind an explicit **"Enhanced voice (downloads ~80MB)"** toggle so first-visit users aren't surprised, and show a progress bar via `progress_callback`. Stream generation sentence-by-sentence using `TextSplitterStream` to start playback within ~500 ms.

Keep Web Speech as the default; add Kokoro as the opt-in upgrade. The **single-voice coherence** principle — one narrator sustained across an entire session, because voice-switching breaks trance — matters far more for hypnosis than for meditation. Pick one Kokoro voice and stick with it within a session; offer a small voice gallery (af_heart, af_bella, am_michael, bf_emma) for cross-session selection.

Beyond TTS, four audio upgrades compound. **Procedural soundscapes in AudioWorklet** (loaded via Blob URL to stay single-file) replace your current 10 presets with infinitely-varied layers: rain as three noise bands plus stochastic impulse drops, wind as bandpass-filtered brown noise with slow LFO gusts, ocean as pink noise under a 0.1 Hz amplitude envelope. **PannerNode HRTF** gives binaural voice drift — narrator orbiting the head on a 20–60 second circle is profoundly dissociative and requires zero libraries. **Theta binaural beats** (carrier 200 Hz, +6 Hz offset, ChannelMerger for isolated channels) have modest but real meta-analytic support (Garcia-Argibay et al., 2019: g = 0.45 across 22 studies; theta specifically enhances hypnotic susceptibility per Brady & Stevens, 2000). **Gain-automation ducking** — drop music to 20% when the narrator speaks, linearly ramp over 300 ms — is ten lines of code and transforms perceived production quality.

## Commercial-app feature gaps a generative tool can close

Commercial hypnosis apps beat you on *depth* but lose on *breadth*, personalization, and cost. Reveri ($99.99/yr), Nerva ($199/yr), Evia (~$99/quarter), and Mindvalley RTT ($399/yr) share the same moat: **structured multi-session programs with state carried across sessions**. Nerva's 6-week IBS protocol is 42 daily 15-minute sessions with symptom tracking, daily readings, and breathing homework. An LLM can generate this: store `{programId, currentDay, totalDays, priorFocus, anchorWord}` in localStorage and feed it into each day's prompt, producing scripts that reference prior sessions ("return to the staircase from yesterday"), progressively deepen induction, and reinforce a consistent anchor.

Ship these seven features in priority order, each uniquely suited to generative AI:

**Intake Q&A → personalized script** (Reveri parity). Three to five pre-session questions — current issue, symptom intensity 0–10, preferred imagery, outcome framing, hypnotizability tier — fed into the generation prompt. This alone replicates what Reveri charges for.

**Condition-specific protocol library** covering the ten verticals commercial apps monetize: IBS/gut (use published Monash gut-directed imagery structure), sleep, anxiety, smoking/vaping, weight/eating, chronic pain, hot flashes/menopause (Elkins' "cool place" protocol), confidence, phobias, sports/flow, HypnoBirthing. Each is a preset prompt with correct clinical framing.

**Multi-day structured programs** — 6-week IBS, 5-week menopause, 8-week confidence — where each day's generation knows the day number, program arc, and prior anchors.

**Session-length modes with distinct structure per mode**: 1–3 min quick-relief (induction-lite + one suggestion + rapid reorient), 5-min starter (the habit-formation sweet spot for beginners), 10-min standard (Reveri's workhorse, the highest-adherence-per-effect length), 20-min deep (unlocked after 10 sessions), and **Sleep mode with no-awakening ending** — HypnoBox's explicit toggle, trivial for an LLM: omit reorientation, add trailing-off suggestions, end in silence. The research is unambiguous: consistency 4–7 days/week matters far more than session length (Cearns & Clark, *JMIR* 2023, 280k sessions).

**User-authored suggestion weaving.** Let users type their own affirmations; the LLM weaves them into deepening and post-hypnotic phases. HypnoBox's core value proposition, delivered natively and without module limits.

**Daily "top-up" micro-doses** (90 seconds) that reference yesterday's anchor ("same floating hand, same word 'calm'"). This alone creates Nerva-style daily cadence.

**Script export + teleprompter mode** with paced highlighting. Lean into the HypnoBox insight that users value recording their own voice. The tool generates, they narrate — this sidesteps the one area where commercial apps genuinely beat you (professional narration warmth).

## Retention design for therapeutic content requires a different playbook

Duolingo's aggressive streak mechanics actively harm therapeutic apps. Duolingo's own head of retention, Jackson Shuttleworth, has warned that bolting streaks onto apps whose core loop doesn't deliver daily value creates hollow engagement. For hypnosis specifically, a streak-anxious user opening a hypnosis tool to avoid losing a streak is counter-therapeutic. Worse, a 2021 JMIR meta-analysis found gamification added **no benefit over non-gamified mental-health apps for depression** — the anhedonia hypothesis in action.

The evidence favors a **Finch-style nurturing model over a Duolingo-style punishment model**. Finch's explicit design — no crying mascots, no red warnings, auto-applied streak freezes, pet states that only move positively — achieved mental-health app leadership without dark patterns. Insight Timer hits **16% Day-30 retention** (industry-leading; Calm/Headspace sit around 8.5%) partly because it leads with autonomy: topic-following, teacher-following, no forced progression. Real-world medians are sobering: median 30-day retention for mental-health apps is 3.3%, with 94% disengagement in the first two weeks (PMC12910276).

Build retention around **Reveri's "rising score"** as the primary motivator. Capture a 1–10 self-rated state before and after every session, plot the delta over time, and display it prominently. This is the Tran et al. d = −0.71 to −0.78 effect, personalized: users see their own therapeutic improvement, which out-motivates any badge system. Layer on these specific mechanics:

Run a **compassionate streak** — count practiced days, auto-apply two freezes per week, offer a 48-hour "earn-back" window to restore a lapsed streak by completing one session. Never use loss-framed copy ("you're about to lose!") or red warnings. Surface a soft "last session: 2 days ago" instead. Include user-scheduled rest days (most adherent users practice 4–5 days/week, not 7).

Show a **GitHub-style calendar heatmap** rather than a trophy cabinet — dense, respectful, scannable, and entirely renderable as inline SVG from a `sessionsByDate` localStorage dict. Milestones at 7/21/50/100/365 sessions as soft notices, not confetti. **No confetti or XP animations for 30 seconds post-session** — let the user emerge from reorientation.

Build an **unlock-by-practice library**: start with 3 inductions visible; unlock others at sessions 3/7/15/30. This is novelty-reward for consistency — intrinsic-adjacent, not performative. Avoid leaderboards, social comparison, and competition entirely; they're incompatible with trance work.

For onboarding, compress to **≤5 screens and <60 seconds to first audio**: goal (single-tap cards), experience level, session length, voice preference, 1-tap state check-in. Anchor practice to an **existing routine cue** ("after morning coffee," "before bed") rather than a clock time — the JMIR 14,879-user Calm analysis found morning + temporal consistency best predicts adherence. Copy the Duolingo finding that changing "Continue" to "Commit to my goal" lifted retention via intentionality.

## Browser-native immersion without a build step

Seven browser capabilities are implementable today in a single HTML file and add disproportionate UX value. **Screen Wake Lock API** is now universal (Firefox added it 2023, Safari 2022), takes four lines, and prevents the screen-sleep failure mode that kills mid-session immersion. **WebGL fragment shaders via glsl-canvas** (`<script src="https://unpkg.com/glslCanvas@latest/dist/GlslCanvas.js">` + `<canvas class="glslCanvas" data-fragment="…">`) deliver Shadertoy-quality spirals, tunnels, plasma, animated Julia sets, and reaction-diffusion patterns at 60 fps on mobile — a dramatic upgrade from Canvas 2D. **SVG breathing guides** with `<animate>` tags give crisp, GPU-composited, accessibility-friendly pulsing orbs.

The `prefers-reduced-motion` media query is non-negotiable — visuals designed to alter states can trigger vestibular disorders, migraine, and seizures. Respect it, but also ship a persistent **always-visible pause/exit button ≥44×44 px** with an Esc keyboard shortcut regardless, per WCAG 2.2 Success Criterion 2.5.8. **No flashing content at all**: WCAG 2.3.1 and ITU-R BT.1702 permit up to 3 flashes/second with area limits, but for trance-induction visuals, static imagery with fades ≥500 ms and breathing-rate animations under 0.25 Hz are safe and sufficient. Run any animation through the free **PEAT tool** from the Trace Center before shipping.

For sleep sessions, add a **warm-overlay sleep mode** — a `position: fixed; inset: 0; pointer-events: none; background: rgba(255,120,30,0.15); mix-blend-mode: multiply` div approximates blue-light reduction. Browsers have no direct color-temperature API (macOS Night Shift is OS-level); the Ambient Light Sensor API is effectively dead outside Firefox Android; use a **time-of-day heuristic plus manual toggle** instead. The Media Session API surfaces title and play/pause on the phone lock screen — users lock their phone mid-session and still have controls. **Page Visibility API** should pause audio and re-request wake lock on tab-return.

Two optional biofeedback features become genuine "killer opt-ins" for users who have the hardware or consent. **Microphone-based breathing detection** via a Web Audio AnalyserNode, thresholding energy in the 100–600 Hz band, can sync narration pacing to the user's actual breath rhythm — entirely local, privacy-clean. No commercial hypnosis app does this. **Web Bluetooth heart rate** from a Polar H10 or Muse headband is roughly ten lines of vanilla JS (Heart Rate Service UUID `0x180D`), works in Chromium browsers, and unlocks session-adaptive physiology feedback. Both must be explicit opt-ins behind a user gesture. Camera-based rPPG (via `heartbeat-js`, validated at ~2.56 bpm error by Di Lernia et al., 2024, *Behavior Research Methods*) is feasible but accuracy degrades in low light — ship it only as an experimental demo.

**A-Frame WebXR** deserves mention as a small-audience, high-ceiling bet. One script tag (`<script src="https://aframe.io/releases/1.7.1/aframe.min.js">`) plus declarative `<a-scene>` HTML gives you a procedural forest or beach environment reachable via an "Enter VR" button that lights up only when `navigator.xr?.isSessionSupported('immersive-vr')` returns true. The evidence base is real — a 2022 systematic review (7 studies, N=798) and a 2024 Mayo Clinic RCT (Healium VR, 100 pre-surgery patients) both favor VR-enhanced relaxation over audio-only. Headset penetration is 1–3% of users but the experience is disproportionately memorable.

Skip the features that don't exist or have died: display brightness APIs (security-blocked), Ambient Light Sensor (effectively removed), reliable SSML in Web Speech (engines strip most tags), Resonance Audio for a single narrator (PannerNode HRTF alone suffices). Also verify **Service Worker scope** on Perchance before committing to offline mode — generator iframes may be sandboxed.

## Safety, disclosure, and ethics — the non-negotiable layer

Digital hypnosis has a real but small adverse-event surface. Reveri's 84,395-user analysis found only **10 users reporting worsening** (all minor) — an excellent safety signal at scale, but achieved through deliberate design. BSCAH's catalog of unwanted effects (Gruzelier et al.) lists drowsiness, body-schema distortion, abreaction, headache, palpitations, stiffness, and rare seizures; Eimer (2012, *Am J Clin Hypn* 55:8) lists populations where self-hypnosis is contraindicated without clinician oversight: active psychosis, acute mania, complex PTSD with flashbacks, Dissociative Identity Disorder, severe personality disorders with unstable reality testing, active suicidality, photosensitive epilepsy, acute intoxication, under-18 without caregiver, severe cognitive impairment. Wieder & Terhune's 2022 meta-analysis in *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews* confirms elevated hypnotic suggestibility in dissociative disorders, reinforcing the DID exclusion.

Implement an 11-item contraindication checklist with explicit attestation at first-run and monthly re-display. Frame the tool as a **wellness self-help aid, never therapy or treatment** — this is the ASCH/SCEH/BSCAH joint-guidelines language and the legally safer framing per Romano Law's 2024 analysis. Cite "based on clinical research," not "clinically proven." Disclose AI involvement conspicuously (Cohen & Slottje, 2024): "Content is generated by AI based on clinical-hypnosis research; it is not reviewed in real time by a human therapist." Include a crisis-resource screen reachable from every state: 988 (US), Samaritans 116 123 (UK/Ireland), Lifeline 13 11 14 (Australia), and an international directory link.

On every session start, display the four non-negotiable disclosures: not a substitute for licensed care; not while driving or operating machinery; photosensitivity warning if animated; all data stays on your device via localStorage. Make them dismissible after first acceptance, redisplayed monthly. Cap sessions at 2–3/day to prevent fatigue and compulsive use. **Never offer trauma-processing or abreaction content** in a self-help tool without clinician oversight — these require live human judgment.

The re-alerting protocol deserves a dedicated implementation. At session end, run a mandatory sequence: fade audio over 2–3 seconds, count up 1→5 with directive eye-opening, suggest stretching fingers and toes, explicitly cancel any session suggestions not in the user's best interest, and re-measure the Howard Alertness Scale. If post-session alertness drops below baseline, auto-trigger a 60-second 5-4-3-2-1 sensory grounding (name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you touch, 2 you smell, 1 you taste) plus a prompt to drink water before letting the user navigate away. Warn against driving for 10–15 minutes post-session. This single protocol — already evidence-based per Howard (2017) and Kluft (2012) — closes the most-commonly-cited safety gap in every app review.

## The prioritized build order

The research converges on a clear sequence. **Ship in this order** (grouped by effort-to-impact):

Week 1–2, universal must-haves: Screen Wake Lock, `prefers-reduced-motion` + persistent pause button, Page Visibility pause, Media Session lock-screen controls, warm-overlay sleep mode, pre/post 1–10 state capture with a rising-score line chart, Howard Alertness Scale wrapper, and a compassionate streak with auto-freezes. All are universal-browser, zero-dependency, and together they transform the tool's perceived quality more than any single feature.

Week 3–5, the killer upgrades: Kokoro-82M with WebGPU-or-WASM fallback behind an opt-in toggle, glsl-canvas WebGL shader visuals replacing the Canvas 2D effects, PannerNode HRTF voice drift, AudioWorklet procedural soundscapes via Blob URL, gain-automation ducking, and the condition-specific protocol library across ten verticals.

Week 6–8, the differentiators: multi-day structured programs with localStorage-held state, session-length modes with sleep-ending toggle, intake Q&A driving generation, user-authored suggestion weaving, daily top-up micro-doses, post-session journaling with pattern surfacing, and the hypnotizability self-screener.

Stretch bets worth exploring after the core ships: microphone breathing sync (genuinely novel, zero commercial competition), Web Bluetooth HRV for Polar H10 and Muse owners, A-Frame WebXR mode, and theta binaural beats behind an "with headphones" toggle. These won't reach most users but define the tool's ceiling.

The overall positioning is honest and defensible: a commercial hypnosis app sells you one voice, one program, one pricing tier for $99–$399/year. **Your tool wins on infinite personalization, private-by-architecture (nothing leaves the browser), zero cost, and rapid regeneration** ("same script but slower," "swap beach for mountain"). It loses on professional narration polish and published RCT evidence. Ship against that positioning: frame outputs as scripts plus teleprompter plus optional TTS, never as finished therapeutic audio, and the comparison to Reveri becomes complementary rather than competitive. The d = −0.75 effect size they report is likely reproducible in a free, private browser tool — if you build the scaffolding the clinical evidence actually points to.
